Timothy Halstead is a historian whose main area of research is public schools in the Great War.

He has written extensively on this topic, as well as on the development and role of the Officer Training Corps both before and during the Great War. His first book was A School in Arms: Uppingham and the Great War and since then More than Victims of Horace: Public Schoolboys 1914-1918 has been published by Helion. His current areas of research are the London Territorials and the London Schools. He is also researching the relationship between London Territorials and the Commonwealth War Graves Commission cemeteries.
